Revitalizing Compassion
The project proposes the redesign of Roger Neilson House's webpage, a palliative care facility in Ottawa. User research, including interviews and surveys, was conducted to gather insights. Competitor analysis highlighted strengths in accessibility standards, but weaknesses were found in certain websites. Heuristic evaluation identified issues with images, contact information, page length, and font size. User perspectives, actions, feelings, and pain points were considered. The problem statement focused on saving user information to streamline the volunteer application process. Sketches were created, and the site map was adjusted to include an 'About Us' section. A UI style guide was developed based on user preferences. Next steps included incorporating features, adding a contact form, and improving accessibility.
Transforming the Digital Frontier
The non-profit organization's website faced issues with cluttered navigation, outdated information, and limited resource access. Steps taken to address the problem included a heuristic analysis and usability testing. Insights led to a redesign of the information architecture, improvements in the footer design, and the creation of mockups and a UI style guide. The resulting solution aimed to provide a clearer purpose, intuitive navigation, and easier access to resources. Future steps involve finalizing prototypes and perfecting the footer design.
Designing Smart Recipes
This case study addresses the problem of people struggling to find new and exciting recipes that cater to their food preferences. The proposed solution is an AI-powered mobile application that generates personalized recipes based on users' dietary preferences, available ingredients, and desired meal types. The app aims to provide a convenient and time-efficient way for users to discover and plan diverse and nutritious meals. By using the app, users can save time, broaden their culinary horizons, improve their nutrition knowledge, and efficiently organize their meal preparations. The focus is on creating a seamless and enjoyable user experience while helping users achieve their goals of learning new recipes, saving money on groceries, trying new dishes, and managing their time effectively.

About

What sets Anika apart is her unique background, characterized by their experience as a residential program manager for adults with developmental disabilities. This valuable experience has instilled in her a profound sense of empathy and a strong motivation to enhance usability and accessibility for all individuals. They firmly believe that technology possesses the power to bridge gaps and offer equitable opportunities, enabling marginalized voices to be heard. Fueled by this passion, her focus lies in creating designs that are not just visually appealing but also inclusive and accessible.
